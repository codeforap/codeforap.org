OUR COMMUNITY

Social Causes

--------------------<<<<<<<<<<<<-------------------------->>>>>>>>>>>>>>-----------------------

The government very well knows the importance of uplifting the society at large as nothing much can be accomplished without their involvement in the administrative process. This is why the immediate focus is on:

  • Initiating eLearning in elementary schools
  • Automating pension benefit payments, farmer markets, cooperating banking and Raitu Bazaars
  • Establishing a grievance redressal system named Meekosam, which means ‘For you’
  • Helping grassroots level farmers about everything related to seeds, weather reports, pesticides, soil tests, availability and water usage
  • Encouraging ‘Adoption of a Village/Ward’ while paying more attention to village level issues

Goals

The Andhra Pradesh government has created ambitious and progressive plans aimed to help with the immediate and long run development of the state. The eventual goal is to change and develop the state so that it ends up the most digitally advanced state in the country, and is also one of the top tourist spots in the world.

Their plan is specifically meant to:

  • Get more than the 100,000 Andhra NRI IT professionals who are located all over the world involved in the project
  • Use Cloud computing and E-seva to help promote E-governance
  • Create a federated digital platform
  • Help develop a platform with a quality ecosystem, capacity and channels around it.

The focus in the near future is mainly on:

  • Developing a knowledge database and analytics
  • Creating open source models like cloud computing and Big Data
  • Developing various services related to social causes

All these programs generally relate to the state’s:

  • Agriculture
  • Healthcare
  • Rural education

History

Andhra Pradesh is the 29th and the newest Indian state and was carved out of the state with the same name on June 20th, 2014 with 13 administrative districts. The Andhra race however dates back to a few centuries BCE and evokes images of classical enlightment and intellectual advancement.

The state has more than 26 100,000 people cities where just two of them boast of a population of more than a million. The state is nicknamed the ‘rice bowl of India’ and boasts of a literacy rate of 67.4% comprising of thousands of professors, engineers, doctors and IT professionals, where most of them work overseas.

All these highly qualified professionals are more than ready and willing to contribute at giving back to their home state in all shapes and forms, as long as they have the opportunity to do so. This very opportunity has now manifested itself in the form of CodeForAP.
